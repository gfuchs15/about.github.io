I work currently for the Fraunhofer Integrated Circuit Institute (IIS) in Erlangen, Germany. My field of research is digital signal processing applied to audio signal and in particular to speech and music compression.


Doctoral Thesis - University of Sherbrooke

January 2003 - December 2006

I completed my doctoral studies between January 2003 and December 2006 at the University of Sherbrooke (Quebec, Canada) in the Speech and Audio Coding Research Group. (GRPA).
The thesis was supported by  the VoiceAge company (Montréal, Québec).

Title: Scalable Audio Coding at Low Bit-Rates

The thesis work focused on the digital audio compression, a technique required for reliable communication of speech and audio. Communication networks tend to increase their capabilities and the integration of services over a shared inter-networks infrastructure based mainly on the IP technology.  The implication for compression technologies is that modern coders have to operate in heteregeneous environments with different capacities and involving different inputs. Consequently, audio encoders need to be able to adapt to varying network conditions and the varying nature of audio content. Scalable coding is one of the solutions that allows such an adaptation. This study gives an overview of the possibilities of scalable audio coding for communication and proposes a practical solution to code speech from as low as 8 kbit/s and make its quality scalable with birate, being able to transmit music from 24 kbit/s while keeping the delay and complexity low.

Keywords : audio and speech coding, successive reffinement and algebraic quantization
