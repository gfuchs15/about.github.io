Postdoctoral Fellowship - Fraunhofer IIS Erlangen

I am currently a postoral fellowship at the Fraunhofer Integrated Circuit Institute (IIS) at Erlangen (Germany). My field of research is the digital signal processing applied to audio signal and especially for speech and music compression.
Doctoral Thesis - University of Sherbrooke

I realised my doctorate studies between January 2003 and December 2006 at the University of Sherbrooke (Quebec, Canada) in the Audio and Speech Research Group (GRPA). The thesis was supported by  the VoiceAge company (Montréal, Québec).

Titel :

Scalable Audio Coding at Low Bit-Rates

The work of the thesis was on the digital audio compression, a technique necessary for the reliability of a communication involving audio. Communication networks  tend to increase their capabilities and the integration of services over a shared inter-networks infrastructure based only on the IP technology.  The implication for compression technologies is that future coders will operate in heteregeneous environments with different capacities and involving different inputs. Thus, audio coders will need the ability  to adapt to varying networks conditions and to the varying nature of the audio contents. The scalable coding permits such adaptation. The scalable audio coding proposed in the work is able to handle convenabely speech at rates from 8 kbit/s, and music from 24 kbit/s while maintenaing low delay and low complexity.

Keywords : audio and speech coding, successive reffinement and algebraic quantization
